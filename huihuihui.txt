club bylaws:

Introduction
This document is designed to guide you as a member of Peshawar Services Club (PSC). It contains important information on bylaws, code of conduct, dress codes, and membership rules. Reading and following these guidelines will ensure that you and your family enjoy the full benefits of the Club while contributing to its respectful, home-like environment.

1. Code of Conduct

PSC is your second home – a place for leisure, interaction, and family gatherings. To maintain harmony and discipline, members are expected to:

• Respect Others: Treat fellow members, families, and staff with courtesy.

• Dress Code: Proper attire maintains Club culture. Members are expected to dress as per guidelines (see Section 4).

• Willful Damage: Penalty up to six times the value of damaged property.

• Personal Belongings: Members are responsible for their own valuables; the Club is not liable for loss/damage.

2. Complaints & Suggestions

• Complaints must be entered in the official Complaint Register (available at particular facility) or submit in writing to the Secretary.

• Constructive suggestions are encouraged to improve facilities and services.

3. Club Finances & Bills

• Monthly Bills: Issued by the 5th of each month (via post/email) and payable by 25th.

• Late Fee: 5% surcharge per month on delayed payments.

• Suspension: Non-payment for four months results in suspension.

• Termination: Non-payment for twelve months results in permanent termination.

4. Dress Code Guidelines

Members are expected to maintain dignity through appropriate attire. Following dress codes for different facilities are as under; 

 

5. Car Parking

• Overnight parking not allowed unless occupying guestrooms.

• PSC not responsible for theft, loss, or damage to vehicles.

• For smooth entry into the club, members can apply for car stickers.

6. Membership Rules

Ineligibility: Persons who are bankrupt, convicted, involved in anti-state/moral turpitude, defaulters, security disqualified, or deemed unsuitable by the Committee cannot be members.

Termination:

- Voluntary resignation (written notice). - Non-payment of dues (12 months). - Misconduct, spreading false information, mutiny, or undermining Club interests.

7. Guests & Visitors

• Foreign Nationals: Not permitted as guests (rooms, dining, parties, meetings, or weddings).

• Affiliated Clubs: PSC members may visit affiliated clubs; facilities vary by agreements.List is available on our website i.e. www.peshawarservicesclub.com

8. General Courtesy

• Supervise children at all times.

• Avoid loud/disruptive behavior.

• Respect prayer timings and designated spaces.

• Smoking only in designated areas.

Conclusion

We warmly welcome you to Peshawar Services Club. By respecting the rules and culture of PSC, you help sustain its reputation as a premier place for families, social interaction, and recreation. Together, let’s keep PSC a true “Home Away from Home.”

banquet hall:

BOOKING PROCEDURE

Event Inquiry & Reservation	 

For booking events such as official/ private banquets, educational seminars, commercial/ arts exhibitions, musical shows, family get-togethers/ parties, birthday parties, mehndi, nikkah, walima, members/ guests are required to approach the booking office at the club or contact the office via official mobile number. The booking incharge will assist with inquiries regarding menu options, venue availability, DJ/ stage setups, and photoshoot arrangements
Menu Selection

The host may choose from the available menu options for buffet dinner, lunch, or Hi-Tea. The host can add additional items to the selected menu
Venue Confirmation

The booking incharge or F&B manager will arrange a venue visit for the host
It is the host's responsibility to communicate seating plans, chair cover themes, and other event-specific details to the booking incharge
Event Registration & Payment

The booking incharge will register the event in the system, including all necessary details (membership number, host name, event date, guest count, contact information, etc.)
A minimum amount set by management committee must be paid as an advance token to the accounts department. Alternatively, the host may choose to clear the full payment during the initial visit
A confirmation receipt will be issued, and an SMS notification will be sent to the host's provided contact number
Media & Security Restrictions

If any media personnel or foreign guests are expected, the host must inform the booking incharge, as Media & foreign guests are not permitted as per club policy
Menu Finalization & Full Payment

The menu and stage setup must be finalized not later than 20 days prior the event. Full payment must be deposited before the event
The booking incharge will inform the designated person at the gate to ensure easy access for guests
Cancellation & Refund Policy

Incase of any valid reason, host may request for cancellation of event by sending a formal letter in the name of Secretary for refund of advance amount. Upon, approval of Secretary, refund for event cancellations will be made according to the following schedule:
Cancellation 20 days or more before the event: 100% refund
Cancellation between 12 - 20 days: 50% refund
Cancellation between 5 - 12 days: 25% refund
Cancellation less than 5 days before the event: No refund
Guest Count & Billing

The F&B Manager or Assistant Supervisor will confirm the number of guests with the party's representative before food service
If the number of guests exceeds the initial count, the member/ guest is responsible for paying the additional amount immediately after the event. If there is any dispute later, the additional charges will be added to the member's bill
The form for additional guests is attached. The host is requested to sign the document and settle the outstanding amount on the same day
Additional Guidelines

Outside food, stage decorations, and DJs are not permitted
All rates include taxes and service charges
Fireworks, firecrackers, and firing are strictly prohibited
Children below 08 years of age will be charged as half buffet rate
Membership number and CNIC copy of guests must be presented at the time of booking
Events must adhere to the club's designated meal times:
Lunch       11:00 AM 3:00 PM
Dinner       7:00 PM 11:00 PM
Exceeding the allotted event time will incur a penalty
During booking miscommunication regarding the guest booking with the reference of member can/ may lead to cancellation/ penalty
Accountability      

Members are responsible for any misconduct or damage to Club property during their event and appropriate action will be taken as per Club policies

club info:

BRIEF HISTORY

Peshawar Services Club (PSC) was founded in 1863 in a Bungalow as Peshawar Club, Which was portion of the present premises and then known as "No. 40, The Mall". The cantonment Bandstand and Children's Playgroup adjoining No. 40 encompassed roughly the site of the present Children's Park. It was purely a social club, open for membership to Europeans and Indians of the gazetted class
A Separate club, known as the Games Club, came into existence a few years later, commissioning three Tennis Courts and two Racquet Courts. On the Racquet Courts was later converted into three Squash Courts. The Games Club had an independent membership and soon included polo in its curriculum, using the present Saddar Polo Ground as its venue. The present Race Course was then a snipe Jheel. The P.V Hunt had its Headquarters in the Peshawar Club
In 1863, the Club members felt the need for a Constitution and framed its initial Rules as a formal Club. The Land with infrastructure was purchased outright. The land was an original grant to the vendor and remains a freehold
In 1899, the Club, after addition of more buildings, a Cricket Ground, a Hockey Ground and Football Ground, extended its membership to 250, which gradually rose to an unlimited number. Nonetheless, the members subscribed to a mandatory Rs. 50 maximum liability and No. 2 Jamrud Road was leased as a Bachelors Quarters' Annexes. The Race course was commissioned, regular races held and a Golf Course and another Polo Ground were added
Following Members got the Club formally registered as Peshawar Club on 3 April 1899, in pursuance of the original Memorandum:    
Brigadier General E.R Elles, K.C.B.C.B., R.A
D.Mc Donalds Esq. E.A., Commissioner
Colonel J.B Lynch, 12th B.C
Colonel H.M Rose, D.S.O' Connel, M.D, R.S.MS
Lieutenant Colonel M.D.O' Connel, M.D, R.S.M.C
Lieutenant Colonel J.C. Ramsay, 24th P.I
Major W.S Marshall, 37th Dogras
Captain A.G Baird Smith, R.S.F
Alan B. Broadwar, Esq., Bar-at-Law
With the outbreak of World War-I, the Games Club ceased to operate independently, therefore, it was merged in the Peshawar Club in 1914. A monthly sum of Rs. 9/- was determined for a married man and his wife, who could avail all the Peshawar Club games facilities. It gradually transformed into a Social and Games Club
After acquiring additional land on lease, the Club buildings were enlarged in 1934 – 35 and in the next two decades, more Residential Quarters, new Dining Rooms, Library and auditorium were added. 1937 saw the opening of a filtered Swimming Pool, while Cinema Club was introduced in 1969 and Guest Rooms were added gradually in later years. An adhoc Gymnasium was started in 1990 and in 1990-91, block of ten new Guest Rooms / Suites was added
For the first time, major works of expansion, renovation and repairs, including the uplift and restoration to tradition/heritage in the building' facade were carried out in 1996 – 97. A Dining Hall tastefully decorated, an expanded Banquet Hall with a stage and bridal room, increasing capacity to 500-600 persons as against the existing 250-300 with beautiful décor, a fully functional Gymnasium and a Barber Saloon were added. A new tube well was sunk, an overhead water tank was constructed and new sewerage lines / septic tanks and cooking area were added. Survey of Club stocks / assets, and conditions of service / membership and general management were all systemized in the periodical meetings of Managing Committee during 1996-97. Peshawar Club was re-designated as "Peshawar Services Club" in in April 2004
PSC continued to be subjected to infrastructural expansion and improvements in the year that followed. A new mosque was added in 2000, Reception at Gate 1 in 2001, Swimming Pool Changing Room in 2003 and a Jogging Track in 2005. Uplifting of main building was carried out in 2006 and a new Generator of 110 KVA was installed the same year. Parachinar Lawn was developed in 2006 on a plot adjacent to the Swimming Pool
In 2007, a new house for the Secretary was constructed. Dhobi Ghat was shifted to its present location and Swimming Pool Lawn was developed sequel to demolishing of the Club Quarters. To meet increasing electricity needs a 200 KVA Transformer was added, old Tombola Hall was converted into a Dining Hall and up-gradation of Banquet Hall was also undertaken this year
Years 2008 and 2009 saw improvements and additions. The lawn in front of Guest Rooms was developed and close circuit cameras were installed in 2008 for improving the security and surveillance arrangements in the Club. Construction of Multi-purpose hall i.e. Shawal Hall was also started in 2008 and it was commissioned the same year. Its first floor with a tin roof was completed and commissioned in 2009
In year 2012 and 2013, swimming pool was renovated, guest rooms also underwent a massive renovation. Engle Bright hall was renovated to modern architecture and was converted to a family hall. Irvine lounge was renovated to a state-of-the art architecture for sitting purposes
In year 2017 and 2018, new building for squash was constructed with state-of-art wooden German flooring courts. New parking sheds were also installed. The badminton courts were also renovated from wooden flooring to synthetic flooring and the roof from CGI sheet to fiber-glass. New gym equipment was also procured during this time. Peshawar services club was also digitized in the same year, software was introduced for all operations, new payment modes were introduced, PSC website and social media were launched. New equipment was also procured for the club’s kitchen. The exterior color theme of the club’s building was also changed from terracotta to white
In year 2019 and 2020, Tin roof marquees (Khyber & Ayub) & Shawal Hall were demolished and renovated to an exquisite modern marquee, restaurants and Salon. Renovation of the guest rooms was carried out. Clay Jogging track was renovated to modern Synthetic jogging track. Ball room underwent a major renovation where the British architecture was revived with a hint of modern interiors. Irvine Lounge was also renovated. Banquet hall was also renovated to the latest exquisite interiors. New building for Staff accommodation was constructed with best amenities. The road inside the PSC premises was also re-carpeted along with newly constructed footpaths
In 2021, Gym was converted into Club café. New gym building was constructed adjacent to the swimming pool. Clay courts for tennis were constructed. The boundary of the club was also redesigned; a new entrance gate was introduced at mall road. A bomb proof boundary wall was constructed towards mall road. Another 250KW electric generator was installed to meet the increasing needs of the club. Exhaust system was installed in the Kitchen. Security and surveillance cameras were revamped to latest technology
In 2022 and 2023, 350 KW Solar Grid system was installed, which provides electricity to the entire club via solar panels. Energy Saving RFID card lock access system was installed in the guest rooms. A new parking area was constructed on Sahibzada Gul road to accommodate the vehicles of guests of Marquee, restaurants and Salon
In 2024, 25-years master plan called "The Club Arena," was initiated to modernize the club's facilities and expand vertically in phases. The first phase introduced separate swimming pools and wellness facilities, including sauna/steam rooms, Jacuzzis, salt rooms, and massage rooms for both ladies and gents, for the first time in club’s history. In addition, a drivers’ waiting area was constructed within the main car parking facility to enhance convenience and order
The construction of Paddle and Pickle Tennis courts has further positioned Peshawar Services Club as the first club in Pakistan to offer all types of tennis courts, including Grass Courts, Red Clay Courts, Synthetic Courts, and Paddle & Pickle Courts
